What You'll Need

SilverRide requirements always meet or exceed the mandatory TNC requirements in your state or locality. If state or local rules exceed the list below, those rules take precedence.

Driver Requirements

  • Age 21 or older (unless your state or locality requires older)
  • Valid U.S. driver's license from the state where you'll be driving, with a clean record.
  • Pass FTA and state drug and alcohol tests where required, and agree to random testing while active in the network.
  • Must be able to complete fingerprinting and a criminal background screening (minimum 7-year review), in accordance with applicable laws and client requirements. Eligibility may be impacted by certain criminal convictions, violent offenses, or inclusion on the National Sex Offender Registry.
  • Drivers interested in a wider range of trip opportunities may choose to complete additional credentialing related to care for older adults, ADA considerations, safety, and other rider support topics. SilverRide offers access to resources that can help drivers expand their qualifications.
  • An iPhone or Android phone for the SilverRide driver app.
  • Willingness to take riders with service animals. The law requires it; we honor it.

Vehicle Requirements

  • Vehicle is generally no more than 10 years old (requirements may vary by state, locality, or partner contract)
  • 4-door sedan or low SUV
  • Current, valid vehicle registration
  • Proof of insurance in your name
  • Ability to complete any state- or market-required vehicle inspections
  • Vehicle should be clean, well-maintained, and free from smoke or other strong odors.

If certain partner or client contracts require a wheelchair-accessible vehicle and you do not currently have one, SilverRide may refer you to leasing options to support those trip opportunities.

Credentialing

Many SilverRide trip opportunities involve riders referred through transit agencies, healthcare organizations, and senior service providers with specific compliance and credentialing expectations.

SilverRide helps drivers understand these requirements and offers access to resources that may support qualification for a wider range of available trips. Specific credentialing requirements vary by partner contract and the trips you choose to take.

California: Health Insurance Stipend (Prop 22)

California drivers may be eligible for a quarterly health insurance stipend under Proposition 22.

To qualify, drivers generally must:

  • Average 15 or more engaged hours per week during the calendar quarter
  • Be enrolled in a qualifying health plan in which they are the subscriber
  • Not be enrolled in an employer-sponsored plan, Medicare, Medicaid/ Medi-Cal, or another non-qualifying government-sponsored plan

Stipend amounts:

Stipend amounts are based on applicable Covered California benchmarks and may be updated periodically. Current payment amounts will be communicated to eligible California drivers.

Quarterly filing windows:

  • Q1 (Jan 1–Mar 31): April 1 - April 15
  • Q2 (Apr 1–Jun 30): July 1 - July 15
  • Q3 (Jul 1–Sep 30): October 1 - October 15
  • Q4 (Oct 1–Dec 31): January 1 - January 15

Drivers should submit their request and proof of qualifying coverage within the applicable filing window through SilverRide's designated submission process. Acceptable documentation may include an insurance card, evidence of coverage, disclosure forms from your insurer, or claim forms confirming active enrollment in a qualifying plan. Stipend payments are processed after the filing window closes and documentation is verified. Drivers with questions about eligibility, required proof, or the status of a stipend request can contact their SilverRide market manager or email Onboarding@SilverRide.com.
